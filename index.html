PR Pathway Guide

Your step-by-step journey from student visa to Australian permanent residency

1

Student Visa (Subclass 500)

Enrol in a CRICOS-registered course. Minimum IELTS 5.5–6.0 required. Health insurance (OSHC) mandatory. Can work 48 hours per fortnight during studies.

Duration: 1–4 years
2

Graduate Temporary Visa (Subclass 485)

After completing your studies, apply for a 485 to gain Australian work experience. Graduate stream (18 months–4 years) or Post-Study Work stream (2–4 years depending on qualification).

Duration: 18 months – 4 years
3

Skills Assessment & English Testing

Obtain a positive skills assessment from relevant assessing body (e.g., ACS for IT, ANMAC for nursing, Engineers Australia). Achieve required English score (IELTS 6.0–7.0 depending on visa).

Allow 3–6 months
4

Expression of Interest (EOI) via SkillSelect

Lodge an EOI online. Accumulate points. Wait for an invitation to apply (ITA). High-demand rounds happen monthly — check cutoff scores regularly.

Ongoing – varies by occupation
5

Permanent Residence Visa (189/190/491→191)

Lodge visa application within 60 days of ITA. Subclass 189 is independent; 190 requires state nomination; 491 is provisional regional (→ 191 after 3 years).

Processing: 6–24 months
Direct Skilled Migration: If you already have overseas work experience, you may not need to study in Australia first. Your occupation must be on the MLTSSL or STSOL list.
1

Check Occupation Eligibility

Verify your ANZSCO occupation is on the MLTSSL (eligible for 189) or STSOL/ROL (eligible for 190/491). The official list is updated regularly.

2

Obtain Skills Assessment

Apply to the relevant assessing body. Provide qualifications, work references, and employment records. Timeline varies from 2 weeks to 6 months.

2 weeks – 6 months
3

Lodge EOI and Receive ITA

Submit EOI in SkillSelect. Invitations are issued in rounds (usually monthly). Minimum 65 points required; competitive scores are 80–90+.

4

Apply for PR Visa

Lodge visa application with 60 days. Pay visa application charge (VAC). Undergo health examinations and police clearances.

Processing: 8–18 months
Regional Advantage: Subclass 491 gives +15 points in EOI and leads to permanent 191 visa after 3 years of regional residence and work.
1

Identify Regional Occupation & State

Your occupation must be on the state's occupation list. Regional areas include all of Australia except Sydney, Melbourne, Brisbane, Perth, and Gold Coast CBDs.

2

Apply for State/Territory Nomination (491)

Apply to a state/territory for a 491 nomination. Each state has its own criteria, job market requirements, and processing timelines. Some states accept EOIs.

2–8 weeks processing
3

Lodge 491 Visa Application

With nomination in hand, lodge the 491 provisional visa. Must have skills assessment and meet English requirements. +15 points effective in EOI.

Processing: 4–12 months
4

Live & Work Regionally for 3 Years

Must genuinely reside and work in a designated regional area. Keep evidence of employment, tax records, and tenancy agreements.

3 years minimum
5

Apply for Permanent Residence (Subclass 191)

After 3 years on a 491, apply for the 191 permanent visa. Must have earned at least $53,900 AUD per year (indexed annually) during the 3 years.

Processing: 5–10 months
Citizenship Timeline: Generally 4–5 years after getting PR, including 12 months as a permanent resident in the 2 years before applying.
1

Obtain Permanent Residency

You must hold a permanent visa (189, 190, 191, or others). The clock for citizenship starts from when your permanent visa is granted.

2

Meet Residence Requirements

Must have been present in Australia for 4 years (including 1 year as a PR in the 2 years immediately before applying). Time on student/work visas counts toward the 4 years.

4 years total presence
3

Citizenship Test & Application

Pass an online citizenship test (Australian values, history, government). Lodge Form 1290 with supporting documents and pay the application fee ($490 AUD).

4

Citizenship Ceremony

Attend an official citizenship ceremony to make the Australian Citizenship Pledge. Receive your Certificate of Australian Citizenship.

Processing: 12–24 months
